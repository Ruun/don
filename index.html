chirped pulseAmplification

Chirped pulse amplification (CPA) is a technique for amplifying an ultrashort laser pulse up to the petawatt level, with the laser pulse being stretched out temporally and spectrally, then amplified, and then compressed again.

More over;


Donna is a professor at the university of waterloo in the department of physics and astronomy. Her main area of focuse is physic. She graduate from McMaster university with a bachelor of engineering degree in engineering physics in 1981. Futhermore, she received a doctor of philosophy degree from the university of Rochester in 1989. For her doctoral research, at the associated Laboratory for laser energetics, supervised by Gerard Mourou. At the time, Mourou worked on the developemnt of an experimental setup that could raise the peak power of laser pulses, to overcome a limitation, that when the maximal intensity of laser pulses reached gigawatts per square centimetre, self-focusing of the pulses severely damaged the amplifying part of the laser. (- wikipedia). it is their work on this that received a noble price in physics in 2018.

Throught this research, she was able to pave the way to the most intense laser pulses ever created. this research have several application in todays industry and medecine.
